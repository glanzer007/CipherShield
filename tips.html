Cybersecurity Awareness

Stay Safe Online

Cybersecurity is everyone's responsibility. Learn how to protect yourself from hackers, phishing attacks, malware and online scams.

Think Before You Click

One careless click can expose your personal information.

Strong Password Tips

Use Long Passwords

Use at least 12–16 characters with letters, numbers and symbols.

Never Reuse Passwords

Each account should have a unique password.

Enable MFA

Multi-Factor Authentication adds an extra security layer.

Password Manager

Use a trusted password manager instead of remembering everything.

Recognize Phishing Attacks

Verify the Sender

Always check the sender's email address. Fake emails often imitate trusted companies.

Inspect Links

Hover over links before clicking. If the URL looks suspicious, don't open it.

Beware of Urgency

Messages saying "Act Now!" or "Your account will be locked" are common phishing tricks.

Internet Safety

Browse the Web Securely

Follow these simple habits to stay protected while using the internet.

Use HTTPS

Visit websites that use HTTPS to encrypt your connection.

Download Carefully

Only download software from trusted and official sources.

Keep Software Updated

Updates fix security vulnerabilities and improve protection.

Use Antivirus

Keep a trusted antivirus program installed and updated.

Protect Your Personal Information

Public Wi-Fi

Avoid accessing banking or sensitive accounts on public Wi-Fi without a VPN.

Privacy Settings

Review your social media privacy settings and share personal information carefully.

Device Security

Lock your phone with a PIN, fingerprint, or face recognition for extra protection.

Top 10 Cybersecurity Tips

  • ✅ Use strong and unique passwords for every account.
  • ✅ Enable Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA).
  • ✅ Keep your operating system and apps updated.
  • ✅ Never share your OTP, PIN, or passwords.
  • ✅ Avoid clicking suspicious emails or unknown links.
  • ✅ Download software only from official websites.
  • ✅ Backup important files regularly.
  • ✅ Use antivirus software and scan your device often.
  • ✅ Log out from public computers after use.
  • ✅ Think before you click or share information online.

Quick Cyber Facts

Phishing

Phishing remains one of the most common cyber threats because it targets human trust rather than software.

Encryption

Modern encryption protects online banking, messaging apps, and secure websites every day.

Cloud Security

Cloud storage should always be protected with strong passwords and MFA.

Awareness

Most cyberattacks can be prevented through awareness and safe online habits.

Stay Alert. Stay Protected.

Cybersecurity is not only about technology—it is also about making smart decisions online. Build safe habits and continue learning to protect yourself and others.
